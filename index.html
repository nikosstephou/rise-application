Cover Letter:


To whom it may concern,

As I am trying to organize my skills and work to present them to you, I’m realising that this is the first job application I’m taking the time for since I moved back to Cyprus a year and a half ago. After spending 8 years abroad (Amsterdam & Berlin) I used my time back on our island to distance myself from the academic environment and sharpen my technical skills and knowledge as a freelance designer. I have been lucky enough to be presented with deeply interesting projects who also had a sufficient economic backbone to support me. Nonetheless, I have realised that I still have the need of a creative environment, may it be for artistic and intellectual reflection between other individuals or the stimulation received through the observation and coexistence with other disciplines. These characteristics I found to be the most inviting and attractive ones in your job listing.

My school, Gerrit Rietveld Academie (GRA) in Amsterdam, has trained me to a conceptual approach of any type of visual problem. I have not been taught any type of software there, neither did it matter which discipline I used to explore my visual approach and research. What it did matter was that my voice as a designer was cultivated and that my problem solving skills had gone through a deeply experimental phase in order to widen the spectrum of my creative capacity. Given that GRA has a half-eon long history in applied arts, I had the chance to experiment with maker spaces, printing and technology workshops. All the software I include in the following list I learned them myself outside the academic boundaries of GRA, underlining my dedication to continuous learning, adaptation to new technologies and acquiring new skills.

In my skills palette, one of my most prominent tools is the entrepreneurship of organising and managing from scratch seven music festivals. Since 2011 I have had the joy to be part of two founding teams (XORKO & Honest Electronics) of such events. I was in charge of implementing human resources, assigning tasks to talented individuals whether that be constructors or artists, managing the budget and fully managing and creating the visual communication such as the online promotion, posters and all visual aspects of a festival. All these experiences had trained me to be a well mannered team player, being able to distance my ego from any type of project and only work towards the best of the team’s goals and efforts as well as the wellbeing of the environment’s atmosphere. About my availability; I have few ongoing projects that are already in a well progressed place and are expected to be finished by mid-July and shouldn’t interfere with a full-time job. What I will need is some time for ‘The Gathering 5’ festival that I’m organising for mid September of 2020 which will take some of my time the first two weeks of the beginning of that month. Other than these I feel ready and have time and energy to take on a full-time job. (keep it in two sentences)

The River Speaks


App Design
UI & UX
Branding
Logo Design


This will be an educational and recreational mobile application on the cultural history of the Pediaios river. It will navigate users along the riverside park from its starting point in downtown Nicosia and will be suitable for use by the general public. It will encourage users to slow down and observe the river environment as well as provide more in-depth information on the history and ecology of the river and its interaction with people through time. The app will include a variety of sensory prompts and activities as well as rich archival visual material.

The mobile application A River Speaks is part of a two-year research project funded by the Sylvia Ioannou Foundation and has been designed by Despo Pasia and Evanthia Tselika

IVAC20


Catalogue Book Design
Visual Communication




Since its very first year, organisers and their associates have sought to showcase experimental cinema, through a truly diverse programme, giving audiences an opportunity to come into contact with the work of different film makers, artistic movements and cinema trends. Their common denominator is the exploration of the aesthetic and formalistic possibilities of audiovisual art, experimentation, social and existential concerns and the questioning of academic film writing. Often, the festival combines film language with other forms of audiovisual expression, such as video-art and animations.

The Festival entitled ‘Images and Views of Alternative Cinema’, is an initiative of the Ministry of Education and Culture and Theatro Ena, in cooperation with the Cultural Organisation Brave New Culture and Point Centre for Contemporary Art. Through its programmes, it aims to highlight experimental and alternative cinematographic expression.

Sickle & Code is an international exhibition, part of Phygital, a programme conceptualised for Cyprus by Dr. Chrystalleni Loizidou within the scope of investigating contemporary movements towards a knowledge-sharing economy that reconsiders capitalist definitions of credit, labour and production. More specifically, in Sickle & Code, Cypriot and international artists present works that reflect, confront and re-evaluate current models of producing under the conjunction of global digital commons of knowledge.

The exhibition was accompanied by several parallel events that include makeathons, workshops, tours for schools and art schools, presentations, talks, performances and screenings."

Sickle & Code is organised by the University of Nicosia Research Foundation as part of project Phygital (an Interreg V 2014-2020 BalkanMed, EU-funded programme) and co-organized in collaboration with the University of Nicosia Fine Arts Programme (Department of Design and Multimedia) and the Municipality of Lakatamia. Orbital


Logo Design/Competition Submission



“Orbital” is a multidisciplinary project in which Nico has created sounds out of shapes. By creating a family of symbols, he proposes a language. These symbols are translated into records, one record for each. The combination of these symbols lead to sound-sentences. By playing four of them at the same time on a quadraphonic sound system, a sonic image of this graphical conversation is created. The performance at Thkio Ppalies lasted for three hours. The crowd was able to walk in and out of the space at any time as the atmosphere was intended for meditation.
